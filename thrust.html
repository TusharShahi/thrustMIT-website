ABOUT US

thrustMIT started off in early 2016 as a small group of rocket enthusiasts with the aim of promoting High-Power-Rocketry in India. The Team intends to take part in the Spaceport America Cup, organized by Experimental Sounding Rocket Association and held in the United States every June. Earlier known as Intercollegiate Rocket Engineering Competition, it is the biggest platform in the world for UG and PG students to showcase their Rocket Engineering skills. With a large participation from the best universities throughout the world and extensive media coverage, it is a huge event thousands of students, researchers and businessmen look forward to. thrustMIT aspires to spread knowledge, promote, foster and bring about technical innovations in the ingenious field of rocketry. The team has come a long way since its inception, and is constantly working towards perfection.

 

SUB SYSTEMS

AERODYNAMICS

The Aerodynamics team works on the fluid interaction of the rocket during flight. It engineers flight stability and drag reduction by working on ideal Nose Cone, Fuselage, Fin and Boat Tail shapes using Computational fluid dynamics.

PROPULSION

Works on the development of the rocket engine, designs the apparatus for performance testing and develops the engine nozzle to optimize specific impulse and thrust outputs.

DESIGN AND SIMULATION

This team works on the structural stability of the entire rocket, while working on all the mechanical components that are housed inside the fuselage and various bays. They also design the ejection mechanism for the parachute and the payload.

MANUFACTURING

Does extensive research on composites. Recommends lighter and stronger materials for enhanced rocket performance. Takes charge for manufacturing and assembling the entire rocket.

ELECTRONICS

Responsible for developing the on-board circuitry by interfacing microcontrollers with sensors, and working with the flight computer algorithms to process various functions.

FLIGHT COMPUTING

The team develops algorithms and runs codes for the payload functioning, detection of the apogee, implementing live telemetry and gathering data for flight statistics.
